Tom Kelly

Tom Kelly had a vision to build an all-in-one fundraising platform for non-profit organizations and schools. Playing the role of a technical cofounder, we've worked closely with him over the last 3 years to create a system that not only drives a sustainable business, but also provide custom solutions that helps to grow and create a fundraising strategy based on customers goals

The Brief

Tom first approached us in January 2013 about his fundraising platform concept. He wanted a website which can act as an online auction platform and a premiere online marketplace which helps to increase donations and bids. This online auction platform would allow business sellers to sell products and items in large quantities to millions of users across USA. Buyers, would then bid in an on-line auction environment on the product listed in individual auctions.

Proving the Concept

In our first meeting with Tom, we both thought that we would be discussing a fundraising platform. However, by the end of the meeting, we concluded that the right place to start was with a custom software to support the unique service aspect of his business. It wasn't perfect, but it didn't need to be — as a proof of concept, it helped him secure capital and begin to run trials with customers, using Facebook to acquire initial users.

A Pleasurable Auction Experience for Customers

With 24Fundraiser’s versatile auction platform your event’s potential is unlimited. For example, our clients have conducted online auctions at galas, concerts, outdoor sports events, school concerts and wine tastings, just to name a few. The only limit is your imagination.The platform is optimized for performance, availability and scalability with unique functionalities of an online auction to manage and track all bidding related activities. We used A/B testing to measure success. We released new versions of the on boarding process to 50% of customers at a time, while the other 50% saw the old version. This allowed us to observe user behavior and determine which version was more effective. In the end, we found that shortening the five-step process to three steps did improve the conversion rate, especially for mobile users.
